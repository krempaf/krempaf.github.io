In Queens, Parking Woes are Plentiful...

The borough has filed more parking complaints than any other in 2019.

By Francesca Krempa // November 15, 2019

Parked cars line the streets in Gowanus, Brooklyn--the historical leader in 311 illegal parking complaints until this year. Now, illegal parking complaints in Queens are higher than any other borough. (Francesca Krempa/NYCity News Service)

New Yorkers are no stranger to parking problems, but Queens residents might have it the worst.

According to NYC Open Data illegal parking 311 complaints throughout the borough have increased over the past five years, trumping the historical leader Brooklyn in 2019 with 2,454 complaints for every 100,000 residents. Data also indicates the most complaints (an average 2,917 per 10,000 people) come from Queens Community Board 5, which encompasses Masbeth, Middle Village, Ridgewood and Glendale.

Why the increase in parking complaints? Yvonne Reddick, District Manager for Queens Community Board 12, believes it’s due to a combination of limited parking opportunities and a lack of enforcement.

“We need stronger parking infrastructure. There’s no free parking in Jamaica and it’s hurting our businesses,” she said. “And consistent enforcement. Not just checking once Mondays and again on Fridays—[legal parking] needs to be enforced every day.”
